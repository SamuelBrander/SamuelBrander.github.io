Time traveler arrives to warn of dire future, refuses to elaborate further, leaves

Locals were surprised when a DeLorean appeared out of thin air in front of them this afternoon. They were taken by even more surprise as its driver apathetically exited the car and shambled over to a supermarket to grab a limited-edition one-litre bottle of a local supermarket's budget option energy drink and as many bars of chocolate as she could carry, explaining that in her future, the supermarket had already removed this caffeinated product from their lineup, and chocolate was no longer a thing as a consequence of the climate crisis. Whilst paying for a truly unholy amount of junk food, she went on to describe how "everything sucks forever" in the future, and how nostalgia from eating junk food from the time period where things were somewhat less terrible was now the only way for her to feel any sliver of joy in life. Clearly tired of the sudden attention of the crowd, she hopped back into her DeLorean and disappeared as quickly as she had arrived, leaving witnesses puzzled.

Sports car driver club demands immediate lifting of traffic laws

A club advocating for the interests of sports car fans and drivers has published an article demanding that all traffic laws be lifted, given that deaths caused by traffic incidents were at an all time low, and that being dictated where you can drive, when, and how fast, was an inappropriate infringement on their civil liberties. Specifically, the club named traffic lights, speed limits, and mandatory seatbelts as key measures they were incontent with. When confronted with the possible increase in injuries or deaths in road accidents, they recommended that traffic safety be shifted more towards individual responsibility of every person choosing to participate in traffic. "There is no absolute safety. We should learn to live with that. If you're paranoid, just stay at home."

Junk food vegan finishes marathon, says he did it "just to flex on the haters"

One particular participant in a local marathon race caught onlooker's attention after finishing the race wearing a shirt with the message "Proud junk food vegan", and replenishing lost calories by devouring an entire can of pringles at a shocking speed. Journalists inquired as to his motivations for running the race, to which the junk food vegan replied:

"Honestly, it was mostly just to flex on the haters. You see, a few years ago I decided to become a vegan, and immediately people started telling me how unhealthy this way of eating was, and how I was going to become all malnourished, weak, and underweight. So I decided to run really far just to flex on them. I started running in the spring after going vegan in the winter, and I realized that my performance improved massively, after only 3 runs I beat my previous distance record of 14 km by running a half marathon. That day, I decided that I wanted to, one day, run a full marathon. So I started training. Oh, and also, vegan junk food is literally my only coping strategy for dealing with my chronic existential crisis stemming from factors like the unresolved climate crisis, and all those calories have to go somewhere."

The interview was then abruptly ended as a crowd of cheering people approached and called for some celebrations, after which the junk food vegan excused himself, saying he felt thirsty for some healthy fermented barley juice.
